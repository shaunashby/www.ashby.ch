Profile


Since childhood I have had an avid interest in science and technology driven by an inquisitive mind. Starting with a fascination for electronics and the home computers of the early 1980s, I found myself drawn to the sciences, achieving a BSc in applied physics, followed by a PhD in high-energy physics (HEP) in 2000.


My doctoral studies led me to the European Laboratory for Particle Physics (CERN) where, until 2007, I worked as a member of staff in the Physics Department. As a member of the software team for the Compact Muon Solenoid (CMS) experiment based at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), I was responsible for managing the tools used in the software development environment. I also contributed to the detector software, written in Object-Oriented C++.


CERN is an international organisation where science and cutting-edge technology meet: the field of HEP relies heavily on modern computing infrastructure and techniques making CERN a fascinating playground for technophiles.


I am currently available for software development or UNIX/Linux systems administration contracts or longer-term positions. Tele-commute would also be interesting. Further details are available in my LinkedIn profile.
